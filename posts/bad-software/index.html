Bad Software

Kinds of bad software:

  • Doesn't work as intended
  • Doesn't work as expected

Note: for my current purposes, 'bad software' is distinct from 'bad code', but they're closely related.

Humility

  • Publicly calling out bad software often does more harm than good
  • There's also the mote and the beam.
    • i.e. I write bad software. Every day.

On expectations:

  • It's possible the user's expectations are bogus
    • ex: The person who thought he was rebooting his computer when in fact he just power-cycled the monitor
  • But even then: good software is good communication, and good communication includes setting expectations

Qualifications

Bad software is a subjective term. So maybe the reason I see it everywhere say more about me than the world.

Bad software is also a relative term.

Reasons

Bad software is everywhere because good software is hard. Good software is hard and rare in the same way that good writing is hard and rare. It's because good thinking is hard. It takes time, intelligence, honesty, humility, empathy, deliberate practice, good taste, and hard work.

If we were to remove all bad writing, there wouldn't be much to read. Abolishing bad thinking would destroy the internet. Certainly it would destroy congress.

So take heart, if you're an intern: skilled professionals with years of experience and big paychecks, they too make bad software. Some habitually, but every one of them at least ocassionally.
