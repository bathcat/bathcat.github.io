Cantanker

A detector for AI-written text. Most detectors fine-tune a language model against a big corpus of human and machine documents, then hand you a number. Cantanker instead measures fourteen structural features of the writing — vocabulary richness, sentence length variation, passive voice ratio, how evenly new words get introduced, how much tone shifts between paragraphs.
The premise is that people are erratic and models are smooth. Humans write lumpy sentences and reach for the same handful of favorite words; an LLM spreads everything out evenly. So most of what it measures is variance, not level.
That trade is deliberate: it's less accurate than a black-box detector, but it shows its work. You get a curve per signal and a highlighted passage showing which parts read as human and which read as machine, and you make the call yourself. Treat the verdict as evidence, not proof.
React on the front, with the analysis running in a Cloudflare Worker.
Try it live.

Chaboo

A game-show style trivia game for a room full of people — or a Zoom room full of people. Players buzz in from their own phones while the host runs the board. I built it for teaching software classes, but it grew past that: there are a pile of pre-built games, and anyone can author their own.
Built with React and RxJS, with game state living in a Cloudflare Durable Object so every browser in the room stays in sync over a websocket.
Play it live.

Sound Mound

A windchime simulator built with React, RxJS, and Tone.js. Drag chimes around the mound, let the wind do its thing, and listen.
